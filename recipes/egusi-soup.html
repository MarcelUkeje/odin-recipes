Egusi Soup

Home

Description

Egusi Soup is a creamy, indulgent West African stew thickened with ground melon seeds (egusi) and enriched with meats, seafood, and leafy greens. The toasted egusi forms tender curds in the rich tomato-and-pepper broth, creating a satisfying, nutty mouthfeel. Traditionally served with swallows like pounded yam, fufu, or eba, this hearty soup is perfect for family meals and festive occasions .

Ingredients

  • 1 cup egusi seeds (melon seeds), blended into a fine powder
  • ½ cup palm oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes and 1 red bell pepper, blended
  • 2–3 Scotch bonnet peppers, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 lb assorted meats (beef chunks, goat meat, or chicken), pre-cooked
  • ½ cup smoked fish or dried shrimp, optional
  • 1 tablespoon ground crayfish
  • 2 bouillon cubes
  • 4 cups water or stock
  • 2 cups chopped leafy greens (spinach, bitter leaf, or ugu)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Prep egusi paste: In a bowl, mix the egusi powder with a little water into a thick paste.
  2. Sauté aromatics: Heat palm oil over medium heat. Sauté onions until soft, add tomato paste, and fry for 2–3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Cook base: Stir in blended tomatoes, bell pepper, and Scotch bonnets. Simmer 10–12 minutes until oil rises to the top.
  4. Season: Add crayfish, bouillon cubes, smoked fish (if using), and pre-cooked meats. Pour in water or stock, bring to a gentle boil.
  5. Poach egusi: Using a spoon, dollop egusi paste into the simmering stew, then gently stir to form curds. Cook 8–10 minutes until soup thickens.
  6. Finish with greens: Add chopped greens, season with salt and pepper, and simmer 3–5 more minutes until wilted.
